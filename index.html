Welcome to my website!

Hi! I'm Lizzie, a linguist-turned-coder based in West Yorkshire.

I set up this website in mid-2023 to document my progress as I learnt to code and started job hunting.

In February 2024 I secured my first role in tech as a Pega Apprentice and have been enjoying learning the low-code Pega platform and settling into my new role.

I’m also a keen salsa and bachata dancer, language lover, and member of Girlguiding.

Keep reading for more details on my background in coding, or visit my Projects page to see the things I’ve been working on.

Background in coding

I think there is a common belief that someone is good at either maths or English, and that they only like one or the other. That was never true for me; I loved both and studied both to A-level. It was always the grammar that I enjoyed most in English because it appealed to my analytical mind and my love of logic (i.e., the reasons I loved maths), so I ended up doing a degree in German and Spanish Linguistic Studies, finding that linguistics was the perfect way to combine my love of logic and languages. After I graduated, I learnt Italian and then started my career in the languages industry, working as a teaching assistant in Spain and Germany and then setting up my own business as a freelance translator.

I first became curious about coding when a friend of mine, who also did a linguistics degree, told me she was now working in cyber security. She told me about her role and I was surprised that something so "techy" could link so well to linguistics. The more I found out, the more it seemed like it would be something I would enjoy. After all, I had already learnt three foreign languages; surely learning a programming language would be no different? If anything, I reasoned, it would be easier, because there probably wouldn’t be exceptions to grammar rules like there are in spoken languages!

So I decided to dip my toe in the world of coding and see what I thought. I started with Python, because I read that it was good for analytical minds. It was a free course and I was doing it very sporadically whenever I had the time. I really enjoyed it, though, so I decided to pay for a short course, knowing I’d be encouraged to do it more regularly if I’d invested money. I did some more reading, and many sources recommended HTML and CSS as a good place to start, so I enrolled on the SheCodes Basics course. I created a simple webpage and was addicted, so I signed up for a year’s membership on Codecademy so I could access a wide range of materials and learn at my own pace. Within the next year, I completed the Front-End course, met a great community of women in tech and secured my first junior developer role. I can’t wait to see where this journey takes me!

On the Projects page of this website, I am documenting everything I've created during my learning journey. Feel free to take a look!
