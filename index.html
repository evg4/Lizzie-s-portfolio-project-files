Welcome to my website!

Hi! My name is Lizzie and I’ve recently discovered my passion for coding.

I have a degree in German and Spanish Linguistic Studies and have since learnt Italian, too. I’ve lived in Cartagena, Spain, and Bremen, Germany, and am now based back in my hometown in West Yorkshire.

I’m currently a self-employed translator and I'm teaching myself to code alongside my work.

Apart from that, I’m a keen salsa and bachata dancer, bookworm (when I get time to read!) and a volunteer for Girlguiding.

Keep reading for more details on my background in coding, or visit my Projects page to see the things I’ve been working on.

Background in coding

I think there is a common belief that someone is good at either maths or English, and that they only like one or the other. That was never true for me as I loved both! But it was always the grammar that I enjoyed most in English because it appealed to my analytical mind and my love of logic (i.e., the reasons I loved maths). I ended up doing a degree in German and Spanish Linguistic Studies, finding that, for me, linguistics was the perfect way to combine my love of logic and languages. After I graduated, I also learnt Italian, and followed a career in the languages industry for a few years.

When I reached a point where I was no longer enjoying my position, I started to have a think about what else I could do, and I recalled how much I had enjoyed linguistics, particularly syntax. After talking to friends who had recently moved into the tech industry with no background in computers, I googled “coding”. The more I read, the more it seemed like it would be something I would enjoy. After all, I had already learnt three foreign languages; surely learning a programming language would be no different? If anything, I reasoned, it would be easier, because there probably wouldn’t be exceptions to grammar rules like there are in spoken languages!

So I decided to dip my toe in the world of coding and see what I thought. I started with Python, because I read that it was good for analytical minds. It was a free course and I was doing it very sporadically whenever I had the time. However, I was enjoying it, so I decided to pay for a short course, knowing I’d be encouraged to do it more regularly if I’d invested money. I did some more reading, and many sources recommended HTML and CSS as a good place to start, so I enrolled on the SheCodes Basics course. It confirmed to me that I definitely wanted to pursue this further, but I wasn’t in a position where I could drop everything and pay for an expensive, full-time course. So instead, I signed up for a year’s membership on Codecademy so I could access a wide range of learning materials and study at my own pace. At the moment, I’m working my way through the Front-End Engineer path. My ultimate goal is to get a career as a developer of some sort.

On the Projects page of this website, I am documenting all the web pages I have created during my learning journey. Feel free to take a look!
