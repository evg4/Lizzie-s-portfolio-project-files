Welcome to my website!

Hi! I'm Lizzie and I’ve recently discovered a passion for coding.

I have a degree in German and Spanish Linguistic Studies and have since learnt Italian, too. I’ve lived in Cartagena, Spain, and Bremen, Germany, and am now based back in my hometown in West Yorkshire.

I’m currently a self-employed translator and I'm teaching myself to code alongside my work.

Apart from that, I’m a keen salsa and bachata dancer, bookworm (when I get time to read!) and a volunteer for Girlguiding.

Keep reading for more details on my background in coding, or visit my Projects page to see the things I’ve been working on.

Background in coding

I think there is a common belief that someone is good at either maths or English, and that they only like one or the other. That was never true for me as I loved both! But it was always the grammar that I enjoyed most in English because it appealed to my analytical mind and my love of logic (i.e., the reasons I loved maths). I ended up doing a degree in German and Spanish Linguistic Studies, finding that, for me, linguistics was the perfect way to combine my love of logic and languages. After I graduated, I also learnt Italian, and since then have been working in the languages industry, most recently as a translator.

I first became curious about coding when a friend of mine, who also did a linguistics degree, told me she was now working in cyber security. She told me about her role and I was surprised that something so "techy" could link so well to linguistics. The more I read, the more it seemed like it would be something I would enjoy. After all, I had already learnt three foreign languages; surely learning a programming language would be no different? If anything, I reasoned, it would be easier, because there probably wouldn’t be exceptions to grammar rules like there are in spoken languages!

So I decided to dip my toe in the world of coding and see what I thought. I started with Python, because I read that it was good for analytical minds. It was a free course and I was doing it very sporadically whenever I had the time. I really enjoyed it, though, so I decided to pay for a short course, knowing I’d be encouraged to do it more regularly if I’d invested money. I did some more reading, and many sources recommended HTML and CSS as a good place to start, so I enrolled on the SheCodes Basics course. I created a simple webpage and was addicted, so I signed up for a year’s membership on Codecademy so I could access a wide range of materials and learn at my own pace. At the moment, I’m working my way through the Front-End Engineer path. Once I learn more, I'd love to create a website for my translation business!

On the Projects page of this website, I am documenting everything I've created during my learning journey. Feel free to take a look!
